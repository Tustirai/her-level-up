Today's Devotion:

"We all make mistakes and have sinned at some point in our lives. It can be challenging to admit our mistakes and take ownership of our sins, but it is necessary for our spiritual growth and well-being.

1 John 1:8-10 (NIV) says, 'If we claim to be without sin, we deceive ourselves and the truth is not in us. If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness. If we claim we have not sinned, we make him out to be a liar and his word is not in us.'

When we confess our sins and take ownership of our mistakes, we can experience the freedom and forgiveness that comes from God. We can also grow in our relationship with Him and become more like Christ. As we seek to follow Jesus, we should ask Him to show us any sin we are denying or are unaware of, so we can confess it and be set free. So, if you are struggling with sin or have made a mistake, remember that God is faithful and just to forgive us when we confess our sins. Don't be afraid to take ownership of your mistakes and ask for forgiveness. As you do so, you can experience the freedom and forgiveness that comes from God, and grow in your relationship with Him."
